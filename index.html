💙 Supporting You Locally

ÉBAN operates on principles of radical transparency and informed consent. Your autonomy matters. Every person we serve understands:

  • What data is collected — Only essential information for care coordination
  • How it's protected — Pseudonymization, encryption, and access controls
  • Who can see it — Only authorized care providers in your network
  • Network sharing — Optional, anonymized, and always voluntary
  • Your rights — Access, correction, and deletion at any time
Core Principle:

People aren't "data subjects" — they're human beings deserving dignity, respect, and full agency over their information.

🌍 Growing Together, Globally

Building collective wisdom — When people consent to network sharing, their anonymized data contributes to collective learning while keeping every individual's identity completely protected and sacred. 💙

🌱 How We Learn Together

When people consent to network sharing, their anonymized data contributes to:

  • Pattern recognition — Identifying systemic issues requiring policy intervention
  • Resource allocation — Understanding where support is most needed
  • Quality improvement — Learning what approaches work best
  • Advocacy — Evidence-based arguments for social change
🌱 Important:

Only the lessons learned (never the raw data) are shared... keeping every individual's identity completely protected and sacred. 💙
